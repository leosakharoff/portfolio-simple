about me project

My journey into programming actually began in the world of jazz. There, I learned to think creatively and navigate the abstract—skills I use today when building interactive installations and working with sound and code.

After doing my bachelor at Sonic College and working a few years as a sound designer for visual media and composing music, I constantly felt the urge to explore the more technical side of things. So I applied for the master's program at ITU—an intensive learning curve in software development that's been a great experience. Now I'm looking for a place where I can combine both worlds: technical curiosity, close collaboration, and building experiences that connect people with space, sound, and technology.

PROJECTS

01 Sound of Chess — Interactive
Sound of Chess explores the intersection of sound and chess through interactive design and generative music. Built with Bela and Pure Data, it uses electrical circuit design and voltage dividers to identify chess pieces, transforming their movements into real-time, evolving soundscapes.
02 Spring Radio — Programming
A community-driven radio project built with local artists and friends. Features a livestream platform with scheduling, cloud storage, and LibreTime integration. A space for collaboration, creativity, and shared soundscapes.
03 Semantic Portal — Programming
Software development and system integration at Semantic ApS. Backend development, APIs, and cloud infrastructure. Building scalable solutions for logistics and business communication with Azure, .NET, and event-driven architectures.
04 Vertigo Installations — Interactive
Interactive artworks built during internship at Vertigo. Raspberry Pi, microcontrollers, TouchDesigner. Custom LED fixtures and lighting systems for art installations.
05 No Latency — Sound Design
Seven years of sound design and music for films, visual media, and commercial clients including Adidas. Spatial audio, real-time processing, generative systems.
